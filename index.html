CAD Modeling

Date – 07.04.2025

Join us for an exhilarating CAD design challenge where participants will put their creativity and skills to the test! In this competition, you'll have the opportunity to showcase your ability. The objective of this challenge is to test participants’ ability to translate a 2D sketch into a fully realized 3D model using CAD SOFTWARE. Participants will need to demonstrate their proficiency in interpreting dimensions, visualizing geometry, and applying MODELING techniques to accurately represent the given sketch in THREE DIMENSIONS.

EVENT DETAILS


• DATE – 07.04.2025


REGISTRATION DEADLINE – 06.04.2025


SOLO

More Info

Here are the detailed rules and guidelines for the CAD Modeling competition:


CHALLENGE DESCRIPTION


• ROUND 1 – Participants will be provided with a 2D sketch depicting a MECHANICAL component; using CAD SOFTWARE, participants must accurately translate the sketch into a detailed 3D MODEL.


• ROUND 2 – Participants will get an “object” and a measuring instrument to make a 3D MODEL of that object. The challenge will test participants' accuracy, creativity, and efficiency in MODELING techniques.


RULES


➢ Any CAD-SOFTWARE can be used for the specific task.

➢ Online sources can be utilized during the specific task.

➢ No faculty will help to perform.

➢ Accuracy: Ensure your 3D model closely matches the dimensions and features of the provided sketch.

➢ Detailing: Focus on capturing essential details while maintaining clarity and simplicity in your design.

➢ Complexity: Select modeling techniques appropriate to the complexity of the sketch.

➢ Time Limit: Complete the 3D modeling task within the allocated time frame.



CHANCE OF WINNING


➢ First priority: Fast and accurate model generation

Draft to Craft

Date – 07.04.2025

“Draft to Craft” in plastic recycling aim to foster innovation, efficiency, and sustainability in recycling processes, products, and policies. They involve creating solutions that are environmentally responsible, economically viable, and scalable for reducing plastic waste in the long run. Plastic recycling focuses on moving from theoretical solutions to tangible, real-world applications that can reduce plastic waste, improve recycling systems, and create sustainable alternatives.

EVENT DETAILS


• DATE – 07.04.2025 (12:15 – 01:00)


REGISTRATION DEADLINE – 06.04.2025


DUO or SOLO

More Info

Here are the detailed rules and guidelines for the Draft to Craft competition:



RULES


Eligibility


• The competition is open to all students of the college.

• Participants can register as individuals or in teams (max 2 members per team).


Theme & Objective


• Participants must create a functional or decorative product using plastic waste.

• The final product should be innovative, eco-friendly, and demonstrate effective plastic waste reuse.


Allowed Materials


• Only plastic waste (Working and non- working model)

• Other supporting materials like glue, paint, thread, or wood can be used.



Process & Timelines


• Idea Submission: Teams must submit a brief proposal.

• The concept & inspiration Materials used Practical use or impact.


Judging Criteria


• Innovation & Creativity – Uniqueness and originality of the craft

• Effective Use of Plastic Waste– How much plastic waste is repurposed

• Functionality– Usability of the product

• Sustainability– Environmental impact of the project


Prohibited Activities


• No pre-made plastic items should be used except waste materials.

• Projects must be safe and non-toxic (no harmful chemicals or unsafe processes).


PRICE & REWARD:

➢ Recognition in front of college faculty & student.
